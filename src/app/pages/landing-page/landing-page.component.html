A Snapshot of the Stars

The Rubin Observatory Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST) is a groundbreaking astronomical survey that will capture an unprecedented view of the universe. Using the state-of-the-art Vera C. Rubin Observatory, LSST will collect vast amounts of data—billions of celestial objects imaged over a decade—to unlock the mysteries of dark matter, dark energy, and the dynamic night sky. To help researchers process this enormous data stream, the Pitt-Google Broker, developed at the University of Pittsburgh, serves as a powerful tool for filtering, classifying, and analyzing LSST alerts in real time. Leveraging Google Cloud and machine learning, this broker enables astronomers to rapidly identify and follow up on transient events such as supernovae, variable stars, and asteroids.
