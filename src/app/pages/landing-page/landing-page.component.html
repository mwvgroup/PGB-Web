A Snapshot of the Stars

The Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST) is a revolutionary astronomical survey that will generate an unprecedented volume of data using the Vera C. Rubin Observatory. Over ten years, LSST will capture billions of celestial objects in repeated wide-field observations, creating a vast dataset to explore dark matter, dark energy, and time-variable phenomena in the universe. With its high-cadence imaging, LSST will produce a continuous stream of alerts, detecting transient events such as supernovae, variable stars, and near-Earth asteroids. Managing and analyzing this immense data flow requires advanced tools to identify and prioritize significant discoveries efficiently.

The Pitt-Google Broker, developed through a collaboration between Google and the University of Pittsburgh, is designed to process and analyze LSST alerts in real time. Built on Google Cloud, it leverages machine learning and scalable computing to filter and classify transient events, helping astronomers quickly determine which detections warrant further study. With advanced querying and data processing capabilities, the broker allows researchers to track and investigate astrophysical phenomena with greater speed and precision.
