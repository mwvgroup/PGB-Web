A Snapshot of the Stars

The Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST) is a groundbreaking astronomical survey that will capture an unprecedented view of the universe. Using the state-of-the-art Vera C. Rubin Observatory, LSST will collect vast amounts of data — billions of celestial objects imaged over a decade — unlocking the mysteries of dark matter, dark energy, and the dynamic night sky. To help researchers process this enormous data set, the Pitt-Google Broker, developed as a collaboration between Google and the University of Pittsburgh, provides powerful tools for filtering, classifying, and analyzing LSST alerts in real time. Leveraging Google Cloud and machine learning, the Pitt-Google broker enables astronomers to rapidly identify and follow up on transient events such as supernovae, variable stars, and asteroids.
